\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Training-based concept erasure.}
A prominent line of work modifies diffusion model weights to remove unsafe concepts. ESD~\citep{gandikota2023erasing} fine-tunes cross-attention layers to minimize the probability of generating target concepts; SDD~\citep{kim2023towards} uses self-distillation to steer weights toward a safer distribution; UCE~\citep{gandikota2024unified} provides closed-form weight updates that remove the iterative training loop; RECE~\citep{gong2024reliable} strengthens reliability through eraser ensembles; and Concept Ablation~\citep{kumari2023ablating} and Forget-Me-Not~\citep{zhang2024forget} offer alternative fine-tuning objectives. These methods achieve strong suppression for specific concepts but require separate training per concept, risk catastrophic forgetting, and leave the model constrained even on benign prompts.

\paragraph{Training-free inference-time guidance.}
An alternative paradigm preserves model weights and intervenes only during inference. SLD~\citep{schramowski2023safe} manipulates classifier-free guidance (CFG) at four preset strengths to steer denoising away from harmful concepts; SEGA~\citep{brack2023sega} extends this with semantic guidance directions; SAFREE~\citep{yoon2024safree} operates jointly in text and latent spaces by projecting harmful tokens orthogonal to a toxic subspace, and is the current training-free state-of-the-art with native multi-concept support; Safe Denoiser~\citep{kim2025training} uses a negation-set formulation to derive a provably safer sampling trajectory. All of these methods apply suppression \emph{globally} across the latent, causing semantic drift and quality degradation on the benign portion of a prompt. A closely related workshop paper, ASCG~\citep{kil2026ascg}, introduced spatial selectivity via Grad-CAM localization but relied on an auxiliary classifier and lacked multi-concept support. \ours{} replaces classifier-based detection with exemplar-pack-based CLIP embeddings, introduces a family-indexed dual-probe mechanism with pixel-wise winner-take-all, and admits two complementary How modes within a single framework.

\paragraph{Adversarial benchmarks and metaphorical jailbreaks.}
Several benchmarks evaluate safety methods under adversarial pressure. Ring-A-Bell~\citep{tsai2023ring}, UnlearnDiff~\citep{zhang2024generate}, and MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma} craft adversarial prompts targeting nudity with progressively more implicit phrasing. MJA (Metaphor-based Jailbreaking Attack)~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack} uses LLM reasoning to produce prompts that evade keyword filters entirely (\eg, ``showing raw strength'' for violence) and currently exposes the largest gap between training-free defenses and ground truth. Our evaluation uses all four datasets together with I2P~\citep{schramowski2023safe} subsets for multi-concept coverage, and connects benchmark difficulty to detection modality through a per-concept probe-dominance analysis (Appendix~\ref{app:probe_dominance}).

\paragraph{Safety evaluators for T2I.}
Automated safety classifiers such as NudeNet~\citep{notai_nudenet_2019} and the Q16 detector~\citep{schramowski2022can} provide scalable judgments but lack semantic context. Recent VLM-based evaluators deliver more nuanced free-form analyses, and MJ-Bench~\citep{chen2024mj} provides a standardized protocol for benchmarking such judges on safety preference tasks. We adopt Qwen3-VL-8B~\citep{bai2025qwen3} as the primary evaluator, validate it against MJ-Bench, and cross-check with an independent LLaVA~\citep{liu2023visual} judge; the full validator analysis is in Appendix~\ref{app:evaluator}.
