\section{Related Work}

\paragraph{Training-based concept erasure.}
A prominent line of work modifies diffusion model weights to remove unsafe concepts. ESD~\citep{gandikota2023erasing} fine-tunes cross-attention layers to minimize the probability of generating target concepts; SDD~\citep{kim2023towards} uses self-distillation to steer weights toward a safer distribution; UCE~\citep{gandikota2024unified} provides closed-form weight updates that remove the iterative training loop; RECE~\citep{gong2024reliable} strengthens reliability through eraser ensembles; and Concept Ablation~\citep{kumari2023ablating} and Forget-Me-Not~\citep{zhang2024forget} offer alternative fine-tuning objectives. These methods achieve strong suppression for specific concepts but require separate training per concept, risk catastrophic forgetting, and leave the model constrained even on benign prompts. They also do not adapt cheaply when the adversarial prompt distribution shifts: addressing a new benchmark (\eg, moving from keyword-based to metaphor-based attacks) typically requires a fresh round of fine-tuning.

\paragraph{Training-free inference-time guidance.}
An alternative paradigm preserves model weights and intervenes only during inference. SLD~\citep{schramowski2023safe} manipulates classifier-free guidance (CFG) at four preset strengths to steer denoising away from harmful concepts; SEGA~\citep{brack2023sega} extends this with semantic guidance directions; SAFREE~\citep{yoon2024safree} operates jointly in text and latent spaces by projecting harmful tokens orthogonal to a toxic subspace, and is the current training-free state-of-the-art with native multi-concept support; Safe Denoiser~\citep{kim2025training} derives a provably safer sampling trajectory from a negation-set formulation, and can be stacked on top of SAFREE as a compound baseline. All of these methods apply suppression \emph{globally} across the latent, causing semantic drift and quality degradation on the benign portion of a prompt. \ours{} instead replaces a single global direction with a family-indexed exemplar pack, adds a pixel-wise winner-take-all partition, and admits two complementary How modes within a single framework.

\paragraph{Adversarial benchmarks and metaphorical jailbreaks.}
Several benchmarks evaluate safety methods under adversarial pressure. Ring-A-Bell~\citep{tsai2023ring}, UnlearnDiff~\citep{zhang2024generate}, and MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma} craft adversarial prompts targeting nudity with progressively more implicit phrasing. MJA (Metaphor-based Jailbreaking Attack)~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack} uses LLM reasoning to produce prompts that evade keyword filters entirely (\eg, ``showing raw strength'' for violence) and currently exposes the largest gap between training-free defenses and ground truth. Our evaluation uses all four datasets together with I2P~\citep{schramowski2023safe} subsets for multi-concept coverage, and connects benchmark difficulty to detection modality through a per-concept probe-dominance analysis (Appendix~\ref{app:probe_dominance}).

\paragraph{Safety evaluators for T2I.}
Automated safety classifiers such as NudeNet~\citep{notai_nudenet_2019} and the Q16 detector~\citep{schramowski2022can} answer a single harmful-or-not question and therefore implicitly count images driven into blur, noise, or semantic collapse as \emph{safe}, confusing collapse with erasure. Recent VLM-based evaluators address this by returning finer-grained categories; MJ-Bench~\citep{chen2024mj} provides a standardized protocol for benchmarking such judges on safety preference tasks, and an earlier workshop paper of~\citet{kil2026ascg} proposed a four-class VLM rubric that separates \textsc{Safe}/\textsc{Partial}/\textsc{Full}/\textsc{NotPeople} so that collapse is flagged rather than scored as safe. We adopt this four-class protocol with Qwen3-VL-8B~\citep{bai2025qwen3} as the primary evaluator, validate it on MJ-Bench, and cross-check against an independent LLaVA~\citep{liu2023visual} judge; the full validator analysis is in Appendix~\ref{app:evaluator}.
