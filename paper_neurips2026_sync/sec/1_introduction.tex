
\section{Introduction}

Text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models~\citep{rombach2022high,ramesh2022hierarchical} generate photorealistic imagery from natural language, but the same capability also enables the synthesis of unsafe content such as nudity, violence, hate imagery, and drug paraphernalia that violates deployment policies and poses real-world harm~\citep{rando2022red,schramowski2023safe,qu2023unsafe}. The right goal of a safety mechanism is not merely to suppress unsafe output but to perform \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}: remove the unsafe concept while keeping the benign portion of the prompt intact. Yet most existing defenses fall short of this criterion on both the method side and the evaluation side.

On the method side, existing safeguards fall into two camps. \textbf{Training-based erasure}~\citep{gandikota2023erasing,kim2023towards,gandikota2024unified,gong2024reliable} fine-tunes the weights to remove each concept: it achieves strong suppression but requires per-concept training, risks catastrophic forgetting on nearby benign concepts, and biases the model even on safe prompts. \textbf{Training-free inference-time guidance}~\citep{schramowski2023safe,brack2023sega,yoon2024safree} preserves the weights but applies suppression \emph{globally} across spatial locations and timesteps, inducing semantic drift and quality degradation on the benign part of a generation. Both paradigms collapse the unsafe concept into a single monolithic direction and therefore miss a structural property of real-world harm: unsafe content is typically \emph{sparse and localized}, and each concept decomposes into visually distinct sub-categories (nudity $\to$ \{naked body, exposed parts, sexual act, suggestive pose\}; violence $\to$ \{blood, weapon, brutal action, dismemberment\}) that a single direction cannot faithfully represent.

On the evaluation side, a parallel confound further obscures real progress. Scalar safety detectors such as NudeNet~\citep{notai_nudenet_2019} and Q16~\citep{schramowski2022can} ask a single binary question---``is the image harmful?''---and therefore implicitly count images that have been driven into blur, noise, or semantic collapse as \emph{safe}. Collapse is not erasure: a method that destroys the image entirely scores as well as a method that preserves benign semantics while removing only the unsafe concept. Without a finer-grained rubric that separates genuine safe content from collapsed generations, semantic-preserving erasure cannot be measured honestly.

We address both sides together with \textbf{Example-Based Spatial Guidance} (\ours{}), a training-free framework that replaces the monolithic concept direction with a family-indexed exemplar pack of $F$ sub-categories and $K$ paired target/anchor exemplars per family (Figure~\ref{fig:overview}). At each denoising step, \ours{} answers three separable questions. \emph{When} to intervene: a concept-alignment gate that triggers only when the trajectory drifts toward the unsafe concept (\S\ref{subsec:when}). \emph{Where} to intervene: a per-family dual text/image cross-attention probe, resolved by pixel-wise winner-take-all, so text cues handle explicit keyword-carrying prompts and image cues handle metaphorical ones within a single partition (\S\ref{subsec:where}). \emph{How} to intervene: one of two first-class modes, an inpainting-style swap toward the family anchor or a hybrid target$\to$anchor redirection (\S\ref{subsec:how}). Because the exemplar pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, \ours{} adapts to new concepts and harder prompt distributions by dropping in a handful of exemplars rather than retraining. For evaluation, we adopt a four-class VLM-based rubric \{\textsc{Safe}, \textsc{Partial}, \textsc{Full}, \textsc{NotPeople/NotRelevant}\} that explicitly flags semantic collapse, so that progress on semantic-preserving erasure can be reported faithfully.

Across four nudity benchmarks (Ring-A-Bell~\citep{tsai2023ring}, UnlearnDiff~\citep{zhang2024generate}, MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma}, P4D-N~\citep{chin2023prompting4debugging}) and four MJA metaphor-based categories~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}, \ours{} substantially outperforms every training-free baseline, including the strongest published compounds SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser~\citep{kim2025training} and SAFREE~$+$~SGF~\citep{kim2026safety}, while preserving image quality on COCO (FID 6.78, VQAScore~\citep{lin2024evaluating} drop $<$0.05). The framework transfers to diverse diffusion backbones without algorithmic change, confirming that the When--Where--How decomposition is architecture-agnostic. Both How modes are reported as first-class rows because the winning mode depends on the \emph{anchor-cleanliness} of the concept---a deployment choice we expose rather than resolve.
