
\section{Introduction}

Text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models~\citep{rombach2022high,ramesh2022hierarchical} can generate photorealistic images from natural language, but the same capacity lets adversarial prompts elicit unsafe content---nudity, violence, hate imagery, drug paraphernalia---that violates deployment policies and poses real-world harm~\citep{rando2022red,schramowski2023safe,qu2023unsafe}. The problem of \emph{concept erasure} asks how to suppress such content at inference without sacrificing the model's usefulness on benign prompts.

Existing safety approaches fall into two families. \textbf{Training-based erasure}~\citep{gandikota2023erasing,kim2023towards,gandikota2024unified,gong2024reliable} fine-tunes the weights to remove each concept; it achieves strong suppression but requires per-concept training, risks catastrophic forgetting of nearby benign concepts, and permanently biases the model even on safe prompts. \textbf{Training-free inference-time guidance}~\citep{schramowski2023safe,brack2023sega,yoon2024safree} preserves the weights and intervenes only during denoising, but current methods apply suppression \emph{globally} across all spatial locations and timesteps, over-correcting benign regions and degrading image quality. We observe that both paradigms collapse the unsafe concept into a single monolithic direction and thereby miss a structural property of adversarial generation: unsafe content is typically \emph{sparse and localized}, and each concept internally decomposes into visually distinct sub-categories (``nudity'' = \{naked body, sexual act, exposed parts, suggestive pose\}; ``violence'' = \{blood, weapon, brutal action, dismemberment\}).

We propose \textbf{Example-Based Spatial Guidance} (\ours{}), a training-free framework that replaces the monolithic concept direction with a family-indexed exemplar pack ($F$ sub-categories, $K$ paired target/anchor exemplars each, Figure~\ref{fig:overview}). At each denoising step \ours{} asks three separable questions---\emph{when} to intervene (a concept-alignment gate that triggers only when the trajectory drifts toward the concept, \S\ref{subsec:when}), \emph{where} to intervene (a per-family dual text/image cross-attention probe resolved by pixel-wise winner-take-all, \S\ref{subsec:where}), and \emph{how} to intervene (one of two first-class modes---an inpainting-style swap toward the family anchor, or a hybrid target$\to$anchor redirection, \S\ref{subsec:how})---all driven by the same pack, with no weight update, no auxiliary classifier, and no gradient step. Because the pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, retargeting, editing, or few-shot adapting a concept reduces to dropping in a directory of prompts and images.

On four adversarial nudity benchmarks (Ring-A-Bell~\citep{tsai2023ring}, UnlearnDiff~\citep{zhang2024generate}, MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma}, P4D-N~\citep{chin2023prompting4debugging}) and the four MJA metaphor-based categories~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}, \ours{} substantially outperforms every training-free baseline---including the strongest published compounds SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser~\citep{kim2025training} and SAFREE~$+$~SGF~\citep{kim2026safety}---while preserving image quality on COCO (FID 6.78, VQAScore~\citep{lin2024evaluating} drop $<$0.05). The framework transfers to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without algorithmic change, confirming that the When--Where--How decomposition is architecture-agnostic. Both How modes are reported as first-class rows throughout, since which mode wins depends on the \emph{anchor-cleanliness} of the concept---a deployment choice we expose rather than resolve.
