
\section{Introduction}

Text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models~\citep{rombach2022high,ramesh2022hierarchical} generate photorealistic images from natural language, but the same capacity lets adversarial prompts elicit unsafe content such as nudity, violence, hate imagery, and drug paraphernalia that violates deployment policies and poses real-world harm~\citep{rando2022red,schramowski2023safe,qu2023unsafe}. The right goal is not simply to suppress unsafe output, but to perform \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}: remove the unsafe concept from a generation while keeping the benign parts of the prompt intact. A parallel evaluator problem compounds the method problem: scalar detectors such as NudeNet~\citep{notai_nudenet_2019} and Q16~\citep{schramowski2022can} only ask ``is the image harmful?'' and therefore implicitly count images driven into blur, noise, or semantic collapse as \emph{safe}. Collapse is not erasure.

Existing safety methods fall into two camps, both of which struggle with this goal. \textbf{Training-based erasure}~\citep{gandikota2023erasing,kim2023towards,gandikota2024unified,gong2024reliable} fine-tunes the weights to remove each target concept and can reach strong suppression, but requires per-concept training, risks catastrophic forgetting on nearby benign concepts, biases the model even on safe prompts, and does not adapt cheaply when the adversarial distribution shifts (\eg, moving from keyword-based to metaphorical prompts). \textbf{Training-free inference-time guidance}~\citep{schramowski2023safe,brack2023sega,yoon2024safree} preserves the weights but applies suppression \emph{globally} across all spatial locations and timesteps, over-correcting benign regions and degrading image quality. Both paradigms collapse the unsafe concept into a single monolithic direction and miss two structural properties of adversarial generation: unsafe content is typically \emph{sparse and localized} and each concept decomposes into visually distinct sub-categories, and adversarial prompts arrive in two regimes---explicit prompts with identifiable keywords (where text cues dominate) and metaphorical prompts whose visually recognizable patterns evade keywords entirely (where image cues dominate).

We propose \textbf{Example-Based Spatial Guidance} (\ours{}), a training-free framework that replaces the monolithic concept direction with a family-indexed exemplar pack of $F$ sub-categories and $K$ paired target/anchor exemplars per family (Figure~\ref{fig:overview}). At each denoising step, \ours{} answers three separable questions. \emph{When} to intervene: a concept-alignment gate that triggers only when the trajectory drifts toward the concept (\S\ref{subsec:when}). \emph{Where} to intervene: a per-family dual text/image cross-attention probe resolved by pixel-wise winner-take-all, so text handles the explicit regime and injected-CLIP image embeddings handle the metaphorical one within a single partition (\S\ref{subsec:where}). \emph{How} to intervene: one of two first-class modes, an inpainting-style swap toward the family anchor or a hybrid target$\to$anchor redirection (\S\ref{subsec:how}). Because the pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, \ours{} adapts to a shifted adversarial distribution by dropping in a few representative exemplars rather than retraining---a practical advantage over fine-tuning methods that need a full training pass per new benchmark.

On four adversarial nudity benchmarks (Ring-A-Bell~\citep{tsai2023ring}, UnlearnDiff~\citep{zhang2024generate}, MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma}, P4D-N~\citep{chin2023prompting4debugging}) and four MJA metaphor-based categories~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}, \ours{} substantially outperforms every training-free baseline, including the strongest published compounds SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser~\citep{kim2025training} and SAFREE~$+$~SGF~\citep{kim2026safety}, while preserving image quality on COCO (FID 6.78, VQAScore~\citep{lin2024evaluating} drop $<$0.05). Evaluation uses a four-class VLM rubric \{\textsc{Safe}, \textsc{Partial}, \textsc{Full}, \textsc{NotPeople}\} so semantic-collapse cases are flagged rather than counted as safe. The framework transfers to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without algorithmic change, and both How modes are reported as first-class rows throughout because the winning mode depends on the \emph{anchor-cleanliness} of the concept.
