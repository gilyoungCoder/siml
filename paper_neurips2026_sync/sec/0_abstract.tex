\begin{abstract}
Recent advances in diffusion models have dramatically improved text-to-image generation, but the same capability also enables the generation of unsafe content such as nudity, violence, and hate imagery. Existing safe-generation methods either fine-tune model weights per concept---which is costly, risks catastrophic forgetting, and biases the model even on benign prompts---or apply blanket inference-time guidance that indiscriminately suppresses the entire image and degrades the benign portion of a generation. To address these challenges, we propose \textbf{\ours{}}, a novel, training-free framework for \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}, driven by a small user-editable exemplar pack. Specifically, at each denoising step, a concept-alignment gate determines whether to intervene, a family-indexed dual text/image cross-attention probe localizes the unsafe region by pixel-wise winner-take-all, and a mask-gated correction modifies only the detected region through one of two complementary modes (anchor inpainting or hybrid guidance). Because the exemplar pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, \ours{} adapts to new concepts and harder prompt distributions by dropping in a handful of exemplars rather than retraining. Empirically, \ours{} achieves \textbf{state-of-the-art} performance among training-free methods for unsafe-concept suppression across eight benchmarks while preserving image quality on COCO, and it transfers to diverse diffusion backbones without design changes, providing a robust and adaptable safeguard for safe visual generation.
\end{abstract}
