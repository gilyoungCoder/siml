\begin{abstract}
Text-to-image diffusion models can produce unsafe content such as nudity, violence, and hate imagery, especially under adversarial prompts. Existing defenses either fine-tune the model and risk catastrophic forgetting, or apply blanket inference-time guidance that degrades benign generations. We propose \textbf{\ours{}}, a training-free framework that erases unsafe concepts using only a small user-editable exemplar pack decomposed into $F$ semantic sub-categories. At each denoising step, a concept-alignment gate triggers a family-indexed dual text/image probe that partitions the unsafe region by winner-take-all, and a mask-gated correction in one of two complementary modes---anchor inpainting or hybrid guidance---modifies only the detected region. On four adversarial nudity benchmarks, four MJA categories, and multi-concept erasure, \ours{} substantially outperforms all training-free baselines while preserving COCO image quality. \ours{} transfers to SD3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without design changes, confirming its architecture-agnostic nature.
\end{abstract}
