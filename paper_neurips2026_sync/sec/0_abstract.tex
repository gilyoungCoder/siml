\begin{abstract}
Text-to-image diffusion models can produce unsafe content such as nudity, violence, and hate imagery, especially under adversarial prompts. The central challenge is not simply to suppress unsafe output, but to perform \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}: remove the unsafe concept while keeping the benign parts of the prompt intact. Existing training-based methods fine-tune the weights for each concept (risking catastrophic forgetting and per-concept training cost), and current training-free methods apply suppression globally across the latent, degrading benign generations. We propose \textbf{\ours{}}, a training-free framework driven by a small user-editable exemplar pack decomposed into $F$ semantic sub-categories. At each denoising step, a concept-alignment gate triggers a family-indexed dual text/image probe that partitions the unsafe region by pixel-wise winner-take-all, and a mask-gated correction modifies only the detected region via anchor inpainting or hybrid guidance. Because the pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, \ours{} adapts to harder prompt distributions by dropping in a few exemplars rather than retraining. Across four adversarial nudity benchmarks, four MJA metaphor-based categories, and multi-concept settings, \ours{} substantially outperforms training-free baselines while preserving COCO image quality, and transfers to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without design changes.
\end{abstract}
