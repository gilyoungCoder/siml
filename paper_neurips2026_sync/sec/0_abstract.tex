\begin{abstract}
Text-to-image diffusion models can be driven by adversarial prompts to produce unsafe content such as nudity, violence, and hate imagery. The goal is not merely to suppress such output but to perform \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}: remove the unsafe concept while keeping benign parts of the prompt intact. Training-based erasure fine-tunes the weights per concept, which is costly and risks catastrophic forgetting. Training-free inference-time guidance avoids retraining but applies suppression globally, degrading benign generations. We propose \textbf{\ours{}}, a training-free framework driven by a small user-editable exemplar pack. At each denoising step, a concept-alignment gate triggers a family-indexed dual text/image probe, and a mask-gated correction modifies only the detected region via anchor inpainting or hybrid guidance. Across four adversarial nudity benchmarks and four MJA metaphor-based categories, \ours{} outperforms training-free baselines, preserves COCO image quality, and transfers to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without design changes.
\end{abstract}
