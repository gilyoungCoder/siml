\begin{abstract}
While text-to-image (T2I) diffusion models achieve remarkable generation quality, they also risk producing harmful content such as nudity, violence, and hate imagery. Existing safety mechanisms either fine-tune model weights per concept---costly, prone to catastrophic forgetting, and biasing the model even on benign prompts---or apply indiscriminate, global inference-time interventions that induce unintended semantic drift and degrade benign generations. To address these challenges, we propose \textbf{\ours{}}, a novel training-free framework for \emph{semantic-preserving concept erasure}, driven by a small user-editable exemplar pack. At each denoising step, a concept-alignment gate determines whether to intervene, a family-indexed dual text/image cross-attention probe localizes the unsafe region by pixel-wise winner-take-all, and a mask-gated correction modifies only the detected region through one of two complementary modes (anchor inpainting or hybrid guidance). Because the exemplar pack is the sole concept-specific artifact, \ours{} adapts to new concepts and harder prompt distributions by dropping in a handful of exemplars rather than retraining. Furthermore, we overcome the limitations of traditional metrics like NudeNet---which fail to assess semantic integrity---by adopting a four-class VLM-based evaluation protocol that explicitly flags semantic collapse instead of counting it as safe. Empirically, \ours{} achieves \textbf{state-of-the-art} performance among training-free methods for unsafe-concept suppression across eight benchmarks while preserving image quality on COCO, and transfers to diverse diffusion backbones without design changes, providing a robust and adaptable safeguard for safe visual generation.
\end{abstract}
