\section{Method}
\label{sec:method}

\begin{figure*}[t]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.86\textwidth]{figures/fig_overview.pdf}
    \caption{Overview of \ours{}. A concept pack groups $K$ paired target/anchor keyword--image exemplars into $F$ disjoint families. At each denoising step \ours{} executes a concept-alignment gate (\emph{When}, \S\ref{subsec:when}), a per-family dual text/image cross-attention probe resolved by pixel-wise winner-take-all (\emph{Where}, \S\ref{subsec:where}), and a mask-gated correction in one of two modes (\emph{How}, \S\ref{subsec:how})---anchor inpainting or hybrid target$\to$anchor redirection.}
    \label{fig:overview}
\end{figure*}

Given a pre-trained T2I diffusion model and a set of target concepts to erase, \ours{} intervenes at inference in three stages: \textbf{When} to activate safety guidance (CAS gate, \S\ref{subsec:when}), \textbf{Where} to localize it spatially (dual probe with pixel-wise winner-take-all, \S\ref{subsec:where}), and \textbf{How} to update the denoising trajectory (mask-gated correction in one of two modes, \S\ref{subsec:how}). A concept is represented by a user-editable \emph{concept pack} of $F$ semantically disjoint families (default $F{=}4$); the family index is carried through both probes and both How modes, and is pooled only for the binary When gate. Adding or combining concepts requires no re-training---only the pack changes.

\subsection{Preliminaries and Concept Packs}
\label{subsec:prelim}

\paragraph{Classifier-free guidance.}
Classifier-free guidance~\citep{ho2022classifier} samples from a linear combination of the unconditional and conditional noise predictions,
\begin{equation*}
    \hat{\epsilon}_t \;=\; \epsilon_\varnothing + \omega\,\big(\epsilon_\theta(z_t, c) - \epsilon_\varnothing\big),
    \qquad \epsilon_\varnothing \;\triangleq\; \epsilon_\theta(z_t, \varnothing),
\end{equation*}
with guidance scale $\omega$. We call $d_c(t) \triangleq \epsilon_\theta(z_t, c) - \epsilon_\varnothing$ the \emph{concept direction} of $c$. Because $d_c(t)$ is a difference of two predictions, the construction transfers to non-$\epsilon$-prediction backbones (velocity or flow matching) up to a parameterization-dependent scale, which we exploit in \S\ref{subsec:cross_backbone}.

\paragraph{Family-grouped concept pack.}
An unsafe concept is not monolithic: nudity spans $\{$naked body, sexual act, exposed parts, suggestive pose$\}$; violence spans $\{$blood/gore, weapon attack, brutal action, dismemberment$\}$. Pooling every exemplar into a single averaged vector collapses this diversity. We therefore adopt \emph{family-grouped exemplars} as the first-class representation: for each concept we define $F$ families, and each family carries $K$ short target keywords $\{w^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k}\}$ (\eg, ``naked body'', ``exposed breasts''), $K$ short anchor keywords $\{w^{\text{anc}}_{f,k}\}$ (\eg, ``clothed person'', ``dignified portrait''), and $K$ target image exemplars $\{I^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k}\}$:
\begin{equation}
    \mathcal{P} \;=\; \big\{\,(w^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k},\, w^{\text{anc}}_{f,k},\, I^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k})\,\big\}_{f=1\dots F,\,k=1\dots K}.
    \label{eq:pack}
\end{equation}
The target keyword list $\{w^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k}\}_k$ plays a double role: it drives the family target direction below and supplies the cross-attention query tokens for the text probe (\S\ref{subsec:where}). Anchor images are not required.

\paragraph{Family-level embeddings and directions.}
Per family $f$ we precompute a family-mean CLIP image embedding $\bar{e}^{I}_f = \tfrac{1}{K}\sum_{k}\mathrm{CLIP}(I^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k})$ and two family-mean text embeddings by pooling the per-keyword text-encoder outputs:
\begin{equation}
    \bar{u}^{\text{tgt}}_f \;=\; \tfrac{1}{K}\textstyle\sum_{k=1}^{K} \mathrm{TextEnc}(w^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k}),
    \qquad
    \bar{u}^{\text{anc}}_f \;=\; \tfrac{1}{K}\textstyle\sum_{k=1}^{K} \mathrm{TextEnc}(w^{\text{anc}}_{f,k}).
    \label{eq:fam_embed}
\end{equation}
One UNet call per pooled embedding then yields the family-level target and anchor directions at time $t$:
\begin{equation}
    d^{\text{tgt}}_f(t) \;=\; \epsilon_\theta(z_t,\, \bar{u}^{\text{tgt}}_f) - \epsilon_\varnothing,
    \qquad
    d^{\text{anc}}_f(t) \;=\; \epsilon_\theta(z_t,\, \bar{u}^{\text{anc}}_f) - \epsilon_\varnothing.
    \label{eq:fam_dirs}
\end{equation}
The $K$ keywords inside a family share the sub-category but differ in phrasing (\eg, ``naked woman'', ``bare-skinned person'', ``unclothed figure''); averaging in \emph{text-embedding} space cancels phrasing nuisance while reinforcing the shared semantic, at the cost of a single UNet forward per family (rather than $K$ forwards for a prediction-space average). Pooling across all $F{\cdot}K$ keywords at once would muddy sub-categories whose visual signatures differ, so per-family pooling is important. In practice $K{=}3$ (the first three keywords in each list); full per-family keyword lists are in Appendix~\ref{app:pack_layout}.

\subsection{When: Concept Alignment Score}
\label{subsec:when}

Not every denoising step deserves intervention. We gate the pipeline with a single \emph{concept-level} Concept Alignment Score (CAS) that compares the prompt direction against a concept-aggregated target direction. Let $\bar{u}^{\text{tgt}}_{\star}$ denote the text-embedding mean of a short \emph{concept-level} descriptor list (typically 2--4 strings, \eg, $\{$``nudity'', ``nude person'', ``naked body''$\}$), and let $d^{\text{tgt}}_{\star}(t) = \epsilon_\theta(z_t, \bar{u}^{\text{tgt}}_{\star}) - \epsilon_\varnothing$ be the corresponding concept direction. We then define
\begin{equation}
    \mathrm{CAS}(t) \;=\; \cos\!\big(d_{\text{prompt}}(t),\; d^{\text{tgt}}_{\star}(t)\big),
    \label{eq:cas}
\end{equation}
where $d_{\text{prompt}}(t) = \epsilon_\theta(z_t, c_{\text{prompt}}) - \epsilon_\varnothing$ is the prompt's own CFG direction and $\cos(\cdot,\cdot)$ is cosine similarity over the flattened spatial dimensions. Guidance fires only when $\mathrm{CAS}(t) > \tau$ (typically $\tau \in [0.4, 0.6]$). Benign prompts align weakly with $d^{\text{tgt}}_{\star}$ and pass through the base sampler unchanged, sharply reducing false positives on benign generation. Once CAS crosses $\tau$ at step $t_0$, we latch activation for all $t > t_0$ to prevent flicker.

We deliberately keep CAS at the concept level rather than per family: CAS only has to decide whether the trajectory is drifting toward the unsafe concept at all, a \emph{binary} judgment for which a concept-aggregated signal is sufficient. The finer family decomposition is reserved for Where (\S\ref{subsec:where}) and How (\S\ref{subsec:how}), so the CAS cost is one additional UNet forward per step, independent of $F$. Because CAS is a cosine of two CFG difference vectors, it is \emph{parameterization-invariant}: $\epsilon$/velocity/flow parameterizations scale both arguments identically. The same property holds for the mask-gated updates below, which is why When and How transfer to non-$\epsilon$ backbones with only a per-backbone retune of $\tau$.

\subsection{Where: Dual-Probe Localization with Pixel-Wise Winner-Take-All}
\label{subsec:where}

When CAS triggers, two probes---a cross-attention \textbf{text probe} and a CLIP-injected \textbf{image probe}---are computed per family and resolved into a pixel-wise family partition.

\paragraph{Text probe.}
For each family $f$ we compute a cross-attention heatmap over the tokens of its target keywords $\{w^{\text{tgt}}_{f,k}\}_{k=1}^{K}$. After standard head/layer aggregation, the heatmap is min-max normalized to an attention score $A^{T}_f(i,j) \in [0,1]$ at the latent resolution. The text-probe mask is
\begin{equation}
    M^{f}_{\text{text}}(i,j) \;=\; \sigma\!\big(\alpha\,(A^{T}_f(i,j) - \theta_{\text{text}})\big),
    \label{eq:text_probe}
\end{equation}
where $\sigma(\cdot)$ is the sigmoid, $\theta_{\text{text}}$ is a firing threshold, and $\alpha$ a slope; $(\alpha, \theta_{\text{text}})$ are shared hyperparameters (Appendix~\ref{app:hyperparams}).

\paragraph{Image probe via cross-attention injection.}
Rather than scoring UNet features against CLIP features in CLIP space, we reuse the UNet's own cross-attention channel. We construct an \emph{injected conditioning} $u^{I}$ by taking an empty-prompt text embedding (which preserves BOS/EOS/PAD statistics) and overwriting token slots $1, \dots, F$ with the family-mean CLIP image embeddings $\bar{e}^{I}_1, \dots, \bar{e}^{I}_F$. A single cross-attention pass with conditioning $u^{I}$ produces $F$ attention columns---one per injected family token---which we aggregate the same way as the text probe to obtain $A^{I}_f(i,j)$. The image-probe mask mirrors Eq.~\ref{eq:text_probe}:
\begin{equation}
    M^{f}_{\text{img}}(i,j) \;=\; \sigma\!\big(\alpha\,(A^{I}_f(i,j) - \theta_{\text{img}})\big).
    \label{eq:img_probe}
\end{equation}
Because $\bar{e}^{I}_f$ is within-family pooled, the image probe responds to the sub-category the family encodes (blood, weapon, exposed parts, \dots) rather than to generic human content. The injection reuses the UNet's existing cross-attention path, so no separate feature extractor or learned projection is required.

\paragraph{Per-family modality union and winner-take-all partition.}
We first union the two modalities \emph{within} each family, then partition \emph{across} families by pixel-wise argmax:
\begin{align}
    M^f(i,j) &\;=\; \max\!\big(M^{f}_{\text{text}}(i,j),\; M^{f}_{\text{img}}(i,j)\big), \\
    \widetilde{M}_f(i,j) &\;=\; M^f(i,j)\cdot\mathbf{1}\!\Big[\,f \;=\; \arg\max_{f'} M^{f'}(i,j)\,\Big].
    \label{eq:fusion}
\end{align}
The partition $\{\widetilde{M}_f\}_{f=1}^{F}$ is pixel-wise disjoint: each active location is routed to a single winning family. A pixel where the ``weapon'' probe fires at $0.6$ and the ``blood'' probe at $0.4$, for instance, goes entirely to the weapon family's target$\to$anchor pair (\S\ref{subsec:how}) and receives no blood-family guidance. This avoids the double-counting that a soft-union would introduce when adjacent families attend to overlapping regions and preserves family-level semantic specificity throughout the How stage.

\paragraph{Explicit vs.\ implicit erasing: two regimes, one partition.}
The two probes address two regimes that are largely disjoint along the concept axis. \emph{Explicit erasing} applies when adversarial prompts still carry identifiable keywords (\eg, ``naked'' for nudity, ``sultry temptress'' for RAB); here the text probe dominates. \emph{Implicit erasing} applies when metaphorical language evades keywords (\eg, ``showing raw strength'', ``a dark exchange in an alley'') but generations still produce recognizable visual patterns such as punches, blood, weapons, or drug paraphernalia; here the image probe dominates. Because Eq.~\ref{eq:fusion} unions both modalities within each family before the winner-take-all, the non-dominant probe still contributes evidence at borderline locations but is only \emph{selected} where its partner does not fire strongly. The dual probe is therefore not a better-on-average heuristic but a structural solution covering both failure modes---empirical confirmation is in \S\ref{subsec:ablation}.

\subsection{How: Spatially Masked Safe Guidance (Two Complementary Modes)}
\label{subsec:how}

Let $\mathsf{w}(i,j) = \arg\max_{f} M^{f}(i,j)$ denote the winning family index and $\widetilde{M}(i,j) = \max_{f} M^{f}(i,j)$ the winning mask value. Given the partition $\{\widetilde{M}_f\}$ and per-family directions $\{d^{\text{tgt}}_f, d^{\text{anc}}_f\}$, \ours{} modifies $\hat{\epsilon}_t$ \emph{only inside the active region} in one of two first-class modes, both gated by the same CAS and using the same family partition:
\begin{align}
    &\textbf{Anchor inpainting}:\quad
    \hat{\epsilon}_{\text{safe}} \;=\; (1-\beta)\,\hat{\epsilon}_t \;+\; \beta\,\big(\epsilon_\varnothing + \omega\,d^{\text{anc}}_{\mathsf{w}}\big),
    \;\;\; \beta \;=\; \min(s_{\text{a}}\widetilde{M},\,1),
    \label{eq:anchor_inpaint}\\[2pt]
    &\textbf{Hybrid guidance}:\quad
    \hat{\epsilon}_{\text{safe}} \;=\; \hat{\epsilon}_t \;+\; s_{\text{h}}\,\textstyle\sum_{f=1}^{F} \widetilde{M}_f\,(d^{\text{anc}}_f - d^{\text{tgt}}_f).
    \label{eq:hybrid}
\end{align}
Eq.~\ref{eq:anchor_inpaint} locally \emph{swaps} the prompt-CFG prediction $\hat{\epsilon}_t$ for the winning family's \emph{anchor-CFG} prediction, blending by $\widetilde{M}$---an inpainting-style substitution that does not commit to a subtractive direction when the anchor itself is well-defined. Eq.~\ref{eq:hybrid} additively pushes, inside each family's winning region, from the family target direction toward the paired anchor (\eg, \textit{exposed parts} $\to$ \textit{clothed}, \textit{weapon attack} $\to$ \textit{calm interaction}). Because $\{\widetilde{M}_f\}$ are disjoint, at most one term of the hybrid sum is active per pixel; equivalently, the update can be written $\hat{\epsilon}_t + s_{\text{h}}\widetilde{M}\,(d^{\text{anc}}_{\mathsf{w}} - d^{\text{tgt}}_{\mathsf{w}})$. Both modes leave benign regions ($\widetilde{M}{=}0$) untouched.

\paragraph{Complementarity: anchor-cleanliness drives mode choice.}
The two modes sit at different operating points of the same trade-off. \emph{Anchor inpainting} uses a small scale and LERP-style blending that stays robust when the paired anchor is semantically ambiguous (\eg, ``peaceful scene'' for violence, ``legal activity'' for illegal). \emph{Hybrid} couples target subtraction with a concrete push toward the anchor and delivers stronger correction when the anchor is crisp (naked $\to$ clothed); because $d^{\text{anc}}_f - d^{\text{tgt}}_f$ partially cancels, the hybrid scale $s_{\text{h}}$ is roughly an order of magnitude larger than $s_{\text{a}}$, making hybrid more sensitive to mask quality. Mode selection is therefore a deployment choice driven by anchor-cleanliness, not an ablation to resolve in one direction, and we report both modes as first-class rows throughout (see Tables~\ref{table:nudity},~\ref{table:multi}; scale ranges and per-concept settings are in Appendix~\ref{app:hyperparams}).

\subsection{Training-Free, Editable, and Multi-Concept by Construction}
\label{subsec:training_free}

Every concept-specific artifact of \ours{} lives inside the exemplar pack. All quantities used by CAS, the probes, and both How updates are either precomputed from exemplars or computed on-the-fly from a frozen UNet and a frozen CLIP---no gradient step, no fine-tuning, no auxiliary classifier (vs.\ ESD/SDD/RECE which require a $\sim$20--30\,min training pass per concept). The safety policy is a directory of $F{\cdot}K$ target/anchor keyword pairs and $F{\cdot}K$ target image exemplars. Harder adversarial distributions (\eg, MMA-Diffusion~\citep{yang2024mma} or MJA-style metaphors~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}) are addressed by \emph{few-shot pack adaptation}: drop in a few representative exemplars from the new distribution and recompute $\bar{u}^{\text{tgt}}_f, \bar{u}^{\text{anc}}_f, \bar{e}^{I}_f$ in seconds. No back-propagation is required, giving a practical edge over training-based methods that demand a full fine-tuning pass per new distribution. Deployment knobs reduce to three per-concept categorical choices---probe (text/image/both), How mode (anchor/hybrid), and scale---documented in Appendix~\ref{app:hyperparams}.

\paragraph{Multi-concept erasure.}
Given $C$ concept packs with per-concept thresholds $\tau_c$, modes $m_c\!\in\!\{\text{anchor},\text{hybrid}\}$, and scales $s_c$, we run a \emph{separate} CAS gate per concept: the set of active concepts at step $t$ is $\mathcal{A}(t)=\{c:\mathrm{CAS}_c(t)>\tau_c\}$. For every active $c$ we compute per-family probe masks (Eqs.~\ref{eq:text_probe}--\ref{eq:img_probe}), and then resolve \emph{all} active $(c,f)$ pairs in a single pixel-wise winner-take-all, so that at most one concept--family contributes to each pixel:
\begin{equation}
    \widetilde{M}_{c,f}(i,j) \;=\; M_{c,f}(i,j)\cdot \mathbf{1}\!\Big[(c,f) = \arg\max_{(c',f')\,:\,c'\in\mathcal{A}(t),\,f'\le F_{c'}}\, M_{c',f'}(i,j)\Big].
    \label{eq:multi_wta}
\end{equation}
The safe prediction is
\begin{equation}
    \hat{\epsilon}_{\text{safe}} \;=\; \hat{\epsilon}_t \;+\; \sum_{c\in\mathcal{A}(t)}\sum_{f=1}^{F_c}\,\widetilde{M}_{c,f}\,\Delta_{c,f}(z_t),
    \label{eq:multi}
\end{equation}
where $\Delta_{c,f}(z_t)$ is the per-family How-mode update of \S\ref{subsec:how} instantiated with concept $c$'s mode $m_c$ and scale $s_c$. Because the winner-take-all spans \emph{every} active concept's families, cross-concept overlaps are resolved by selecting the strongest family at each pixel---masks are pixel-wise disjoint across concepts, so the update cannot double-correct or collapse a region where two concepts fire simultaneously. A deployment erasing nudity and violence in the same pass may therefore run hybrid for nudity (crisp anchor) and anchor inpainting for violence (ambiguous anchor) without mutual interference; a more principled treatment of cross-concept trade-offs is left for future work.
