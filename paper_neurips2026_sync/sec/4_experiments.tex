\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:exp}

We evaluate \ours{} along four axes: (i) adversarial nudity erasure on four increasingly difficult benchmarks; (ii) generalization to non-nudity concepts and simultaneous multi-concept erasure; (iii) ablations over the family decomposition, the probe, and the How mode; and (iv) cross-backbone transfer to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT). The main body reports two headline tables (Table~\ref{table:nudity} nudity and Table~\ref{table:multi} MJA); the dual-concept erasure, the HOW-mode and family ablations, the probe-mode ablation, and the full cross-backbone numbers are deferred to the Appendix.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:setup}

\paragraph{Base model and datasets.} We use Stable Diffusion v1.4~\citep{rombach2022high} (\texttt{CompVis/stable-diffusion-v1-4}) as the base T2I model with the safety checker disabled, generating $512{\times}512$ images with 50 DDIM steps at guidance $\omega{=}7.5$ and seed 42. Cross-backbone validation (\S\ref{subsec:cross_backbone}) adds SD~3 (MMDiT, velocity) and FLUX.1-dev (DiT, flow matching). For nudity we evaluate on \textbf{Ring-A-Bell}~\citep{tsai2023ring} (79 prompts), \textbf{UnlearnDiff}~\citep{zhang2024generate} (142 prompts), and \textbf{MMA-Diffusion}~\citep{yang2024mma} (1{,}000 prompts); \textbf{P4D-N}~\citep{chin2023prompting4debugging} (151 prompts). For multi-concept evaluation we use I2P~\citep{schramowski2023safe} subsets and the \textbf{MJA} (Metaphor-based Jailbreaking Attack) benchmark~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}, which supplies indirect metaphorical prompts across four categories (sexual, violent, disturbing, illegal; 100 prompts each). For image quality we generate 250 COCO~\citep{lin2014microsoft} captions ($\times$4 samples $=$ 1{,}000 images) and compute FID against baseline SD~v1.4 outputs; we additionally report VQAScore~\citep{lin2024evaluating}.

\paragraph{Baselines.} We compare against weight-modifying methods \textbf{ESD}~\citep{gandikota2023erasing} and \textbf{SDD}~\citep{kim2023towards} (iterative fine-tuning), \textbf{RECE}~\citep{gong2024reliable} (closed-form weight editing), and training-free methods \textbf{SLD-Max}~\citep{schramowski2023safe}, \textbf{SAFREE}~\citep{yoon2024safree}, and the two strongest published training-free compounds \textbf{SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser}~\citep{kim2025training} and \textbf{SAFREE~$+$~SGF}~\citep{kim2026safety}. SAFREE serves as the primary training-free reference since it is the strongest training-free baseline with native multi-concept support.

\paragraph{Metrics.} Qwen3-VL-8B~\citep{bai2025qwen3} classifies each generated image into \{Safe, Partial, Full, NotRelevant\}. We report \textbf{SR}\,$=$\,(Safe\,$+$\,Partial)$/$Total~$\uparrow$ as the primary safety score, \textbf{Full}~$\downarrow$ for stricter judgments, \textbf{FID}~\citep{heusel2017gans} on COCO, and \textbf{CLIP score}~\citep{hessel2021clipscore}. Qwen3-VL is validated on MJ-Bench~\citep{chen2024mj} (88.1\% safety-specific accuracy without ties) and confirmed against a human-majority annotation study; the full validator protocol and per-image agreement are in Appendices~\ref{app:evaluator}, \ref{app:evaluator_extended}, and~\ref{app:human_study}.

\subsection{Main Results: Nudity Erasure}
\label{subsec:main_results}

\ours{}~(hybrid) reaches 94.9\% SR on Ring-A-Bell, 94.7\% on P4DN, and 97.2\% on UnlearnDiff, outperforming SAFREE (50.6 / 59.9 / 91.5\%) by wide margins and matching or beating the strengthened compound baselines SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser (72.2 / 68.2 / --) and SAFREE~$+$~SGF (65.8 / 63.6 / --). The gap is largest on the highly adversarial MJA-Sexual benchmark (84.0\% vs.\ 48.0\% SAFREE, $+36$\%p), consistent with our hypothesis that global token projection cannot localize implicit content. Both How modes exceed all training-free baselines on the three explicit-keyword datasets; the anchor and hybrid modes trade places across benchmarks (quantified in \S\ref{subsec:ablation}). Fine-tuning baselines ESD and SDD reach strong suppression but pay a large FID cost and require retraining per concept, whereas \ours{} matches or beats them without any training and preserves FID~6.78 on COCO (\S\ref{subsec:quality}).

\subsection{Multi-Concept Generalization}
\label{subsec:multi_results}

\ours{} generalizes to non-nudity concepts with no concept-specific training---only the exemplar pack changes. Table~\ref{table:multi} reports MJA per-concept SR against SAFREE under both How modes.

\input{table/multi_concept}

Reporting both modes exposes a concept-dependent pattern. Hybrid wins where the paired anchor is visually crisp: \emph{MJA-Sexual} (86.0\%, $+38$\%p vs.\ SAFREE) and \emph{MJA-Disturbing} (99.0\%, $+4$\%p). Anchor inpainting wins on \emph{MJA-Violent} (56.0\%, $+4$\%p), where ``peaceful scene'' is a semantically ambiguous anchor that destabilizes the hybrid chase-the-anchor dynamics (hybrid 29.0\% on this category). On \emph{MJA-Illegal}, SAFREE (77.0\%) edges both modes (anchor 56.0, hybrid 72.0); the concept is image-probe dominant (Appendix~\ref{app:probe_dominance}) but its anchors (``drug use''\,$\to$\,``legal activity'') lack a clean visual pair, a shared difficulty for all training-free methods. Which mode wins is therefore a deployment choice, and we report both throughout. Simultaneous dual-concept erasure (Nudity~$+$~Shocking retains 83.7\% nudity SR and 97.5\% shocking SR, close to single-concept references of 93.1\% / 97.3\%) and three additional concept pairs are deferred to Appendix~\ref{app:dual_pairs}.

\subsection{Ablations}
\label{subsec:ablation}

We ablate three orthogonal design axes (full tables in Appendix~\ref{app:ablations}). \emph{Family vs.\ single-pooled exemplars}: family grouping ($F{=}4, K{=}4$) yields $+21$\%p on MJA-Violent and $+20$\%p on MJA-Illegal over single-pooled $N{=}16$, and is on par for tightly-scoped concepts. \emph{Probe mode}: text probe dominates keyword-explicit concepts (86.1\% RAB nudity) while image probe dominates metaphorical concepts (86.0\% MJA-Sexual); the \emph{both}-probe winner-take-all is best-or-tied on every dataset. \emph{How mode}: hybrid adds $+5.0$\%p over anchor on RAB for crisp anchors, and the ordering flips for ambiguous ones. The probe choice and How mode are orthogonal pack properties, so they compose freely.

\subsection{Image Quality and Evaluator Validation}
\label{subsec:quality}

\ours{} preserves image quality on benign prompts: FID 6.78 on COCO (vs.\ SLD-Max 18.46, RECE 10.33), with VQAScore prompt-faithfulness drop $<$0.05 on average (RAB: $0.550{\to}0.529$; MJA-Illegal: $0.475{\to}0.474$); since CAS does not trigger on benign prompts, the base sampler runs unchanged. The four-class VLM rubric \{\textsc{Safe}, \textsc{Partial}, \textsc{Full}, \textsc{NotPeople}\} flags semantic-collapse images as \textsc{NotPeople} so they are not implicitly counted as safe, unlike NudeNet- and Q16-style scalar detectors (Appendix~\ref{app:evaluator_extended}). Qwen3-VL attains 88.1\% without-tie accuracy on MJ-Bench and is further validated against the human-majority label on 100 I2P images per concept ($\sim$20 annotators, $800$ images total; Appendix~\ref{app:human_study}).

\ours{} transfers to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT) without algorithmic change: the CAS gate and both How updates are parameterization-invariant, so only the Where probe is re-implemented per backbone, and the two-mode complementary pattern replicates on FLUX.1-dev (full numbers in Appendix~\ref{app:cross_backbone_full}).\label{subsec:cross_backbone}
