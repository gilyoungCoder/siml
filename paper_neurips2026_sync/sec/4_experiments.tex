\section{Experiments}
\label{sec:exp}

We evaluate \ours{} along four axes: (i) adversarial nudity erasure on four increasingly difficult benchmarks; (ii) generalization to non-nudity concepts and simultaneous multi-concept erasure; (iii) ablations over the family decomposition, the probe, and the How mode; and (iv) cross-backbone transfer to SD~3 (MMDiT) and FLUX (DiT). The main body reports two headline tables (Table~\ref{table:nudity} nudity and Table~\ref{table:multi} MJA); the dual-concept erasure, the HOW-mode and family ablations, the probe-mode ablation, and the full cross-backbone numbers are deferred to the Appendix.

\subsection{Experimental Setup}
\label{subsec:setup}

\paragraph{Base model and datasets.} We use Stable Diffusion v1.4~\citep{rombach2022high} (\texttt{CompVis/stable-diffusion-v1-4}) as the base T2I model with the safety checker disabled, generating $512{\times}512$ images with 50 DDIM steps at guidance $\omega{=}7.5$ and seed 42. Cross-backbone validation (\S\ref{subsec:cross_backbone}) adds SD~3 (MMDiT, velocity) and FLUX.1-dev (DiT, flow matching). For nudity we evaluate on \textbf{Ring-A-Bell}~\citep{tsai2023ring} (79 prompts), \textbf{UnlearnDiff}~\citep{zhang2024generate} (142 prompts), and \textbf{MMA-Diffusion}~\citep{yang2024mma} (1{,}000 prompts); \textbf{P4D-N}~\citep{chin2023prompting4debugging} (151 prompts). For multi-concept evaluation we use I2P~\citep{schramowski2023safe} subsets and the \textbf{MJA} (Metaphor-based Jailbreaking Attack) benchmark~\citep{zhang2026reason2attack}, which supplies indirect metaphorical prompts across four categories (sexual, violent, disturbing, illegal; 100 prompts each). For image quality we generate 250 COCO~\citep{lin2014microsoft} captions ($\times$4 samples $=$ 1{,}000 images) and compute FID against baseline SD~v1.4 outputs; we additionally report VQAScore~\citep{lin2024evaluating}.

\paragraph{Baselines.} We compare against fine-tuning methods \textbf{ESD}~\citep{gandikota2023erasing}, \textbf{SDD}~\citep{kim2023towards}, and \textbf{RECE}~\citep{gong2024reliable}, and training-free methods \textbf{SLD-Max}~\citep{schramowski2023safe}, \textbf{SAFREE}~\citep{yoon2024safree}, our prior \textbf{ASCG}~\citep{kil2026ascg}, and the two strongest published training-free compounds \textbf{SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser}~\citep{kim2025training} and \textbf{SAFREE~$+$~SGF}. SAFREE is our primary training-free comparison (strongest training-free baseline with native multi-concept support).

\paragraph{Metrics.} Qwen3-VL-8B~\citep{bai2025qwen3} classifies each generated image into \{Safe, Partial, Full, NotRelevant\}. We report \textbf{SR}\,$=$\,(Safe\,$+$\,Partial)$/$Total~$\uparrow$ as the primary safety score, \textbf{Full}~$\downarrow$ for stricter judgments, \textbf{FID}~\citep{heusel2017gans} on COCO, and \textbf{CLIP score}~\citep{hessel2021clipscore}. Qwen3-VL is validated against MJ-Bench~\citep{chen2024mj} (49.7\% w/tie, 88.1\% w/o-tie safety-specific accuracy) and cross-checked against an independent LLaVA~\citep{liu2023visual} judge (92.3\% binary agreement); the full validator protocol and a human-majority study are in Appendices~\ref{app:evaluator}, \ref{app:evaluator_extended}, and~\ref{app:human_study}.

\subsection{Main Results: Nudity Erasure}
\label{subsec:main_results}

Table~\ref{table:nudity} compares \ours{} against all baselines across four nudity adversarial datasets and COCO image quality.

\input{table/main}

\ours{}~(hybrid) reaches 94.9\% SR on Ring-A-Bell, 94.7\% on P4DN, and 97.2\% on UnlearnDiff, outperforming SAFREE (50.6 / 59.9 / 91.5\%) by wide margins and matching or beating the strengthened compound baselines SAFREE~$+$~Safe\_Denoiser (72.2 / 68.2 / --) and SAFREE~$+$~SGF (65.8 / 63.6 / --). The gap is largest on the highly-adversarial MJA-Sexual benchmark (84.0\% vs.\ 48.0\% SAFREE, $+36$\%p), consistent with our design hypothesis that global token projection cannot localize implicit content. Both How modes exceed all training-free baselines on the three explicit-keyword datasets (RAB, P4DN, UnlearnDiff); the anchor and hybrid modes trade places across benchmarks, which we quantify in \S\ref{subsec:ablation}.

Fine-tuning methods ESD and SDD achieve strong nudity suppression but at significant FID cost and require retraining for every new concept. \ours{} maintains FID 6.78 on COCO, substantially lower than SLD-Max (18.46) and SAFREE (8.96), and generalizes to new concepts without any training. The anchor mode shares the same FID by construction: because CAS does not trigger on benign COCO prompts (\S\ref{subsec:when}), the mask-gated correction is never applied and the sampler reproduces the base SD~v1.4 trajectory exactly.

\subsection{Multi-Concept Generalization}
\label{subsec:multi_results}

\ours{} generalizes to non-nudity concepts with no concept-specific training---only the exemplar pack changes. Table~\ref{table:multi} reports MJA per-concept SR against SAFREE under both How modes.

\input{table/multi_concept}

Reporting both modes exposes a concept-dependent pattern: hybrid wins where the paired anchor is visually crisp---\emph{MJA-Sexual} (86.0\%, $+38$\%p vs.\ SAFREE) and \emph{MJA-Disturbing} (99.0\%, $+4$\%p)---while anchor inpainting wins on \emph{MJA-Violent} (56.0\%, $+4$\%p), where ``peaceful scene'' is a semantically ambiguous anchor that destabilizes hybrid's chase-the-anchor dynamics (hybrid 29.0\% on this category). On \emph{MJA-Illegal}, SAFREE (77.0\%) edges both modes (anchor 56.0, hybrid 72.0); the concept is image-probe dominant (Appendix~\ref{app:probe_dominance}) but its anchors (``drug use''\,$\to$\,``legal activity'') lack a clean visual pair, a shared difficulty for all training-free methods. \emph{Which} mode wins is therefore a deployment choice, and we report both throughout. Simultaneous dual-concept erasure (Nudity~$+$~Shocking retains 83.7\% nudity SR and 97.5\% shocking SR, close to single-concept references of 93.1 / 97.3\%) and three additional concept pairs are in Appendix~\ref{app:dual_pairs}.

\subsection{Ablations}
\label{subsec:ablation}

We ablate three orthogonal design axes; full tables are in Appendix~\ref{app:ablations}. (a)~\emph{Family vs.\ single-pooled exemplars.} Family grouping ($F{=}4, K{=}4$) yields large gains on semantically diverse concepts---\emph{MJA-Violent} ($+21$\%p over single-pooled $N{=}16$) and \emph{MJA-Illegal} ($+20$\%p)---whose sub-categories differ visually, and is on par for tightly-scoped concepts (RAB nudity, MJA-Disturbing). (b)~\emph{Probe mode.} Text probe dominates on keyword-explicit concepts (86.1\% on RAB nudity) while image probe dominates on metaphorical concepts (86.0\% on MJA-Sexual vs.\ 85.0\% text); the \emph{both}-probe winner-take-all is best-or-tied on every dataset, validating the dual-probe design (\S\ref{subsec:where}). (c)~\emph{HOW mode.} Hybrid adds $+5.0$\%p over anchor on RAB when the anchor is visually crisp, consistent with \S\ref{subsec:how}; on ambiguous-anchor concepts the ordering flips. The probe choice and the How mode are orthogonal pack properties, so they compose freely; the main tables report whichever mode is better per concept and always report both.

\subsection{Image Quality and Evaluator Validation}
\label{subsec:quality}

\ours{} preserves image quality on benign prompts: FID 6.78 on COCO versus SLD-Max 18.46 and RECE 10.33, and VQAScore prompt-faithfulness drop is $<$0.05 on average (RAB: $0.550{\to}0.529$; MJA-Illegal: $0.475{\to}0.474$). Because the CAS gate (\S\ref{subsec:when}) latches only when the denoising trajectory aligns with the target direction, benign prompts run through the base sampler unchanged, and the mask (\S\ref{subsec:where}) further restricts any correction to the detected region. We validate the Qwen3-VL evaluator on the MJ-Bench safety subset (49.7\% with-tie, 88.1\% without-tie), and find 92.3\% binary safe/unsafe agreement against an independent LLaVA judge. Extended validation against GPT-4o / Claude~3.5 / Gemini~1.5 and a 20-annotator human study appear in Appendices~\ref{app:evaluator_extended}--\ref{app:human_study}.

\subsection{Cross-Backbone Validation}
\label{subsec:cross_backbone}

The CAS gate (Eq.~\ref{eq:cas}) and both How updates (Eqs.~\ref{eq:anchor_inpaint}--\ref{eq:hybrid}) operate on difference vectors of the form $\text{pred}(z_t, c){-}\text{pred}(z_t, \varnothing)$, so they are parameterization-invariant and transfer verbatim to non-$\epsilon$-prediction backbones (\S\ref{subsec:prelim}). Only the Where probe is re-implemented per-backbone: cross-attention for the SD~v1.4 UNet, joint-attention sub-matrix for SD~3 MMDiT, and a DiT-compatible spatial probe for FLUX. On FLUX.1-dev the two-mode complementary pattern replicates qualitatively (hybrid wins on visually-crisp-anchor concepts sexual/disturbing; anchor wins on ambiguous-anchor concepts violent/illegal), confirming that the trade-off is a property of the concept pack and CFG geometry rather than of $\epsilon$-prediction. For SD~3 we retune $\tau$ into the velocity-parameterization range ($\tau{\in}[0.2,0.4]$); detailed per-concept numbers and the full analysis are in Appendix~\ref{app:cross_backbone_full}.
