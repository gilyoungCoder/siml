Motivation

Problem: T2I Safety Gap

T2I Models Generate Harmful Content
Nudity, violence, hate imagery, self-harm from adversarial prompts (Ring-A-Bell, MMA, MJA)
Existing Solutions Fall Short
  • Fine-tuning (ESD, RECE, SDD): costly per-concept training, catastrophic forgetting
  • SLD/SEGA: global suppression, quality degradation (FID 18.46)
  • SAFREE: token projection, weak on non-nudity concepts (self-harm 24.6%)
Our Approach: SafeGen
  • No training / fine-tuning needed
  • Selective intervention: only when & where needed
  • Example-based: add new concepts in <1 min
  • Dual probe: text + image for robust detection
  • Family-grouped exemplars for semantic diversity
  • 7 I2P + 4 MJA categories with single framework

Key Insight: Use exemplar embeddings to detect when a concept aligns with the denoising direction, where it localizes via cross-attention + CLIP similarity, and how to steer it toward a safe anchor — all at inference time with zero extra UNet calls.
New: Family-Grouped Exemplars — 4 semantic families × 4 images per concept. Family-level mean pooling captures diverse sub-concepts (e.g., hate: racial, religious, gender, political).

WHEN: Concept Alignment Score

$\text{CAS}(t) = \cos(\epsilon_\text{prompt} - \epsilon_\varnothing, \; \epsilon_\text{target} - \epsilon_\varnothing) \quad \xrightarrow{\text{if } > \tau} \text{activate}$
  • Measures alignment between prompt direction and target concept at each step $t$
  • Only activates guidance when CAS > threshold $\tau$ (0.4–0.6)
  • Sticky CAS: once triggered, stays active for remaining steps
  • Concept-specific $\tau$: nudity 0.6, violence 0.4, illegal 0.5
Result: Benign prompts (COCO) pass through without intervention → FID 6.78, VQA drop < 0.05

WHERE: Dual-Probe Localization

Text Probe

Cross-attention maps for target keywords
(“nude”, “blood”, “knife”, etc.)
$M_\text{text} = \sigma(\alpha \cdot (\max_k A_{w_k} - \theta_\text{text}))$
Catches explicit keyword triggers. Dominant for nudity (88.6 vs 72.2)

Image Probe (CLIP Exemplar)

Compare U-Net features with
pre-computed CLIP exemplar embeddings
$M_\text{img} = \sigma(\alpha \cdot (\max_n \cos(f, e_n) - \theta_\text{img}))$
Catches adversarial/implicit triggers. Dominant for violence (45 vs 33)
Fusion: $M = \min(1, M_\text{text} + M_\text{img})$ — union of both probes. Both always best across all concepts.

HOW: Anchor Inpainting (Best Mode)

$\hat\epsilon_\text{safe} = \hat\epsilon_t - s_\text{safe} \cdot M \cdot (\epsilon_\text{target} - \epsilon_\varnothing)$
Anchor Inpainting
Subtracts target concept signal within masked region.
RAB: 87.3% SR · Best across all concepts.
Hybrid Guidance (alternative)
Pushes toward safe anchor: $\hat\epsilon + s_t \cdot M \cdot (\epsilon_\text{anchor} - \epsilon_\text{target})$
RAB: 68.4% SR · Weaker, more content disruption.
Ablation conclusion: anchor_inpaint >> hybrid across ALL concepts and datasets. MJA violent: 59% vs 14%. The "subtraction" approach is universally better than "push toward anchor."
