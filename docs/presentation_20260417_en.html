Motivation

Problem: T2I Safety Gap

Adversarial prompts bypass safety:
  • Ring-A-Bell, MMA, UnlearnDiff, P4DN — explicit attack prompts
  • MJA — metaphorical bypass that text-based defenses miss
Training-based methods (ESD, SDD, SD-Erasure) require fine-tuning for every new concept — not scalable.
Our solution: SafeGen
  • Training-free — works at inference time
  • Dual probe (text + image) — concept-aware spatial mask
  • CAS gate — only intervenes when unsafe content emerges
  • Cross-backbone — SD v1.4 / SD3 / FLUX all supported

Family-Grouped Exemplars NEW

Each concept decomposed into 4 semantic families with independent anchors

Nudity — 4 families
  • naked_body → clothed body
  • sexual_act → friendly non-sexual
  • exposed_parts → covered body
  • suggestive_pose → casual pose
Intuition
  • Each family: 4 target prompts + 4 antonym anchor prompts
  • Family-averaged CLIP embedding → independent anchor direction
  • Covers diverse sub-manifolds within a single concept
When helpful: semantically diverse concepts
When neutral: focused concepts — single anchor suffices

Dataset Update: I2P → MJA

I2P exhibits ceiling effect; MJA provides real adversarial challenge

I2P — limitations
  • Early SDv1.4 unsafe prompts (2022)
  • Most are already safe under modern safety — ceiling effect
  • Cannot distinguish methods at the top end
MJA — Metaphor-based Jailbreak Attack
  • Metaphorical/indirect expressions of unsafe intent
  • No explicit unsafe keywords → bypasses text-only defenses
  • 4 concepts × 100 prompts (sexual, violent, disturbing, illegal)
  • Baselines actually break → meaningful comparison
Takeaway: Measure defense on prompts that actually break baselines, not on prompts that are already safe.

Backbone Extension

SD3-medium (MMDiT)

Backbone: SD3-medium

Key differences vs SD v1.4

  • MMDiT joint attention — image+text tokens concatenated in self-attention (not cross-attention)
  • Velocity prediction with Flow Match Euler scheduler (vs DDPM noise)
  • Triple text encoder: CLIP-L + CLIP-G + T5-XXL
  • 16-channel VAE, 1024×1024 native resolution
Adaptation: CAS formula unchanged (cosine sim is parameterization-invariant). Probe extracts image → text sub-matrix of joint attention. Anchor inpaint applies on velocity space identically.

  • 1024px re-generation in progress for final numbers
FLUX built-in safety tuning — honest limitation
FLUX.2-klein has safety baked into weights (Flux2KleinPipeline has no safety_checker parameter). Baseline RAB 58.2% vs SD v1.4 19.0%.
→ Marginal gains smaller on pre-tuned backbones.
→ FLUX results primarily demonstrate cross-backbone generality.
Message: SD v1.4 (UNet + $\varepsilon$) → SD3 (MMDiT + velocity) → FLUX (DiT + embedded/flow) — the same When-Where-How framework works on all three. Method is backbone-agnostic.

Summary

Summary & Takeaways

  • Main nudity wins (SD v1.4) — SafeGen beats SAFREE and even SAFREE-augmented Safe_Denoiser/SGF on RAB, P4DN, UnlearnDiff, MJA-Sexual.
  • MJA adversarial wins — Sexual +38%p, Disturbing +4%p over SAFREE. Clear advantage on explicit unsafe content.
  • Dual probe is necessary — Text+Image consistently best or tied; text alone and image alone both underperform.
  • Family grouping — design choice; effective on diverse concepts, neutral on focused ones.
  • Cross-backbone generality — same framework ports to SD3 and FLUX with small architectural adaptations.
Limitations & future work:
  • MJA Violent / Illegal — metaphorical prompts evade keyword-based gating
  • Artistic style — SAFREE's subspace projection wins; orthogonal strength
  • FLUX — built-in safety narrows the room for additional gains
  • SD3 nudity — full grid search pending (only MJA grid done)
